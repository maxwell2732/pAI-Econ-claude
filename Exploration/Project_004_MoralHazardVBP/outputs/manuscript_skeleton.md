## Title
Drug Procurement, Moral Hazard, and Originator Exit: A Theoretical Framework

## Paper Structure

**Introduction** (~1.5 pages)
- Economic phenomenon: China VBP achieves 50% price reductions; motivation is cost containment
- Thesis: cost containment intuition is incomplete; three channels (moral hazard amplification, originator exit, insurer-planner wedge) undermine simplistic cost-reduction logic
- Main findings: P1 (expenditure tipping), P3 (complementarity), P4 (welfare kink, central result), P6 (insurer-planner wedge)
- Model ancestry: Pauly (1968) / Zeckhauser (1970) extended with originator participation constraint

**Section 2 — Model** (~1.5 pages)
- Agents: insurer I, originator O (c_o, F>0), generic G (c_g, no fixed cost), N patients
- Timing: insurer sets p → originator decides {participate, exit} → patients demand D((1-λ)p)
- Preferences: patients quasi-linear; insurer objectives (cost-minimizing vs. welfare-maximizing)
- Notation: full table
- Equilibrium: Constrained Optimization with Rational Expectations (Stackelberg)

**Section 3 — Main Results** (~6 pages, 6 propositions)
- Lemma 1 (P1): Expenditure tipping at ε = 1
- Proposition 1 (P2): Existence of exit threshold p̄
- Proposition 2 (P3): ∂p̄/∂λ < 0 (complementarity, counterintuitive) ★
- Proposition 3 (P4): Welfare kink Ω at p̄ (central result) ★★
- Proposition 4 (P5): Second-best price p^SB = c/(1-λ)
- Proposition 5 (P6): Insurer-planner wedge; corner solution for inelastic demand

**Section 4 — Discussion** (~1 page)
- Reform sequencing: expand λ before deepening VBP (P3 implication)
- When is originator exit welfare-improving? (P4 criterion; GQCE equivalence)
- VBP overshooting: competitive bidding drives p below p^SB (P5/P6)
- EC2 nuance: generous insurance reduces Ω (two competing forces)
- Scope limitations: dynamic R&D, physician incentives, GE, heterogeneity

**Section 5 — Conclusion** (~0.5 pages)
- Summarize central result and counterintuitive finding
- Policy prescriptions table
- Future work
